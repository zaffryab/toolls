What is SSL?

SSL (Secure Sockets Layer) is a security protocol that creates an encrypted connection between a web server and a web browser. It ensures that all data passed between the server and browser remains private and secure.

Why SSL is Important

SSL protects sensitive information such as login credentials, credit card numbers, and personal data. It helps prevent hackers from stealing information and builds trust with your website visitors.

How Often to Check SSL

It's recommended to check your SSL certificate at least once a month. Certificates typically expire after 1-2 years, and an expired certificate can cause security warnings for your visitors.
