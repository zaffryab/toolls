How to Use the Loan Calculator Tool

This tool helps you calculate loan payments, total interest costs, and see a complete amortization schedule. Follow these steps:

Step 1: Enter Loan Details

Input the following information:

  • Loan Amount: The total amount you're borrowing
  • Annual Interest Rate: The yearly interest rate percentage
  • Loan Term: The number of years to repay the loan
  • Start Date: Optional - when your loan payments begin
  • Extra Monthly Payment: Optional - any additional amount you plan to pay each month

Step 2: Calculate

Click the "Calculate Loan" button to see your results.

Step 3: Review Results

The calculator will show you:

  • Monthly Payment: Your estimated monthly principal and interest payment
  • Total Interest: The total amount of interest you'll pay over the life of the loan
  • Total Payment: The total amount you'll pay (principal + interest)
  • Payoff Date: When your loan will be fully paid off
  • Amortization Schedule: A detailed table showing how each payment is applied

Understanding Amortization

Loan amortization is the process of paying off a loan over time through regular payments. Each payment covers both interest and principal:

  • Interest: The cost of borrowing money, calculated as a percentage of the remaining balance
  • Principal: The portion of the payment that reduces your loan balance

In the early years of a loan, a larger portion of each payment goes toward interest. As the loan balance decreases, more of each payment applies to the principal.

Example Calculation

For a $250,000 loan at 4.5% interest for 30 years:

  • Monthly Payment: $1,266.71
  • Total Interest: $205,917.22
  • Total Payment: $455,917.22
  • Payoff Date: 30 years from start date

Tips for Saving Money

  • Make extra payments when possible to reduce principal faster
  • Consider a shorter loan term to save on interest
  • Shop around for the best interest rates
  • Make bi-weekly payments instead of monthly to make an extra payment each year
